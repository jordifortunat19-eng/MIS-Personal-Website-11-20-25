Discover UMD & Duluth

Why I chose the University of Minnesota Duluth

Why I Chose UMD

My connection to Duluth and UMD started long before I was a student here. Growing up in Burnsville, my family would often take trips up to Duluth during the summer. Those childhood road trips along the North Shore created some of my fondest memories—watching the ships come into the harbor, exploring Canal Park, and feeling the cool breeze off Lake Superior. Even as a kid, there was something special about this city that stuck with me.

When I was a junior in high school at Apple Valley, I started seriously thinking about where I wanted to go to college. I knew I wanted to study business and marketing, and I wanted to stay in Minnesota but experience something different from the Twin Cities. My parents suggested we take a trip to tour UMD, and I'm so glad we did. The moment I stepped onto campus, I could picture myself here. The campus had this perfect balance—it felt like a tight-knit community, but it was also part of a vibrant city. The business program had exactly what I was looking for, and I loved that UMD had strong connections to the business community in Duluth and beyond.

What really sealed the deal for me was the atmosphere. Walking around campus, I saw students who seemed genuinely happy to be there. The professors I met during the tour were approachable and passionate about their subjects. As someone who values both academics and community, UMD felt like the perfect fit. Now, as a sophomore here, I can confidently say that my instincts were right—UMD has provided me with incredible opportunities, great professors, and a community that feels like home.

What I Love About Duluth

Duluth has so much to offer, and I've grown to love it even more since becoming a student here. The natural beauty is incredible—Lake Superior is right there, and you can't beat the views from campus or the hills around the city. Whether it's taking a walk along the lakewalk, watching the ships come in, or just enjoying the fresh air, there's always something beautiful to experience.

The city has this unique character that I really appreciate. Canal Park is one of my favorite spots—it's perfect for grabbing food with friends, watching the Aerial Lift Bridge, or just hanging out. The local restaurants and coffee shops have become my go-to study spots, and I love how the community supports local businesses. There's always something happening in Duluth, from festivals to concerts to outdoor activities, which keeps life interesting even when I'm not in class.

What I love most is how Duluth feels like a real community. People are friendly, and there's a sense that everyone looks out for each other. Coming from the Twin Cities, it's refreshing to be in a place where you can walk around and actually recognize faces. The city has everything you need, but it's not overwhelming. It's the perfect size for a college student—big enough to have opportunities and things to do, but small enough to feel connected and comfortable.

Campus Highlights & Personal Experiences

My time at UMD has been filled with great experiences both in and out of the classroom. The Labovitz School of Business and Economics has been fantastic—the professors are knowledgeable and really care about student success. I've especially enjoyed my marketing classes, which have given me practical skills I can apply to my career goals in sports marketing.

One of my favorite things about campus is the sense of community. Whether it's studying in the library, grabbing coffee between classes, or hanging out in the student center, there's always a friendly atmosphere. The campus is beautiful, especially in the fall when the leaves change, and I love how everything is walkable. The Kirby Student Center has become a hub for me—it's where I meet up with friends, study, and sometimes just relax between classes.

Balancing school with work at Qdoba taught me a lot about time management, and I'm grateful for the support I've received from professors and advisors when I needed to manage both. UMD has given me the opportunity to grow not just academically, but as a person. I'm excited to continue my journey here and see where my Business Management and Marketing education takes me.
